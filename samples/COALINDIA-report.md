### Ben Graham — 🟡 neutral (40%)

The stock presents a mixed picture through the lens of Benjamin Graham's principles. On the positive side, earnings have been consistently positive across all available periods, and the company has paid dividends in the majority of reported years, which aligns with Graham's preference for a stable dividend record as an extra safety measure. The current ratio of 1.86 is moderately strong, though it falls just short of Graham's preferred minimum of 2.0 for defensive investors. However, the debt ratio of 0.58 is somewhat high, indicating a degree of financial leverage that may introduce risk. Critically, the valuation analysis provides no margin of safety: the net current asset value (NCAV) does not exceed the market cap, ruling out a net-net opportunity, and the Graham Number cannot be computed due to missing or non-positive EPS or book value data. Furthermore, earnings did not grow from the earliest to the latest period, which fails Graham's requirement for stable or growing earnings over multiple years. Without a clear discount to intrinsic value or strong financial strength, I cannot recommend a bullish stance, but the consistent earnings and dividend record prevent a bearish conclusion. Thus, a neutral signal is warranted with low confidence due to insufficient data for a definitive Graham-style assessment.
